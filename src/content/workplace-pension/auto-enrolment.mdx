**Auto-enrolment** is the UK's system for automatically putting eligible workers into a workplace pension. Since 2012, millions more people are now saving for retirement.

## What is Auto-Enrolment?

Auto-enrolment means your employer must:
1. **Assess** whether you're eligible
2. **Enrol** you into a qualifying pension scheme
3. **Contribute** at least 3% of your qualifying earnings

<Callout type="info" title="It's Automatic">
You don't need to do anything to be enrolled. If you're eligible, your employer must set everything up for you.
</Callout>

## How to Opt Out

If you decide auto-enrolment isn't for you:

### The Process

1. **Get the opt-out form** from your pension provider (details in your enrolment letter)
2. **Complete and submit** within one month of enrolment
3. **Get refund** of any contributions already taken

<Callout type="warning" title="Think Carefully">
By opting out, you lose:
- **Free employer contributions** (3%+ of qualifying earnings)
- **Tax relief** (government top-up on your contributions)
- **Years of investment growth**

For most people, staying in is the better choice.
</Callout>

## Re-Enrolment

If you've opted out, your employer must:

- **Re-assess** you every 3 years
- **Re-enrol** you if still eligible
- **Notify** you when this happens

You can opt out again each time, but the process resets.

## Your Rights

### Employers Must:

- Enrol eligible workers automatically
- Contribute at least 3%
- Not encourage opting out
- Not treat you differently for being in the pension
- Keep paying into your pension while employed

### Employers Cannot:

- Fire you for joining the pension
- Reduce your pay because of pension contributions
- Pressure you to opt out
- Use misleading information about the scheme

<Callout type="info" title="Protection">
If your employer pressures you to opt out or treats you unfairly because of pension membership, you can complain to The Pensions Regulator.
</Callout>
