**Pension drawdown** lets you take flexible income from your pension while keeping your money invested. It's the most popular way to access defined contribution pensions.

## What is Pension Drawdown?

Drawdown (also called flexi-access drawdown) is a way to take income from your pension pot while leaving it invested. Unlike an annuity, you don't convert your pot to a guaranteed income - you manage it yourself.

## How Drawdown Works

### Step 1: Move Into Drawdown

When you access your pension:
1. Transfer your pot (or part of it) into a drawdown account
2. Take up to 25% as tax-free cash if desired
3. The rest stays invested

## Sequence of Returns Risk

<Callout type="warning" title="Early Losses Hurt Most">
If markets fall early in your drawdown while you're withdrawing, your pot takes a double hit. This is "sequence of returns risk" - the order of returns matters as much as the average.
</Callout>

### Managing Sequence Risk

- Keep 1-3 years of income in cash/bonds
- Reduce withdrawals in down markets if possible
- Consider a "bucket strategy"
- Don't start drawdown at a market peak if avoidable

### Phased Drawdown

Instead of moving all your pot at once:
- Crystallise in stages over several years
- Spread your 25% tax-free entitlement
- Keep some pot growing without drawdown charges
