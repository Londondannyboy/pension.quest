A **workplace pension** is a retirement savings scheme set up by your employer. Since 2012, most UK workers are automatically enrolled into one. This guide explains how workplace pensions work and how to make the most of yours.

## What is a Workplace Pension?

A workplace pension is a defined contribution (DC) scheme arranged by your employer. You and your employer both pay into it, and the money is invested to grow over time for your retirement.

<Callout type="warning" title="Think Twice Before Opting Out">
Opting out means losing:
- Free employer contributions (3%+ of your salary)
- Tax relief on contributions (20-45% depending on tax rate)
- Years of investment growth

For most people, these benefits far outweigh the small increase in take-home pay.
</Callout>

### Your Options at Retirement

When you reach pension age, you can:

1. **Take 25% tax-free** - The rest taxed as income
2. **Buy an annuity** - Guaranteed income for life
3. **Use drawdown** - Flexible withdrawals
4. **Take it all** - Cash out entirely (tax implications)
5. **Leave it invested** - Continue growing
