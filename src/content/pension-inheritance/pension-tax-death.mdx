## Inheritance Tax: Usually Exempt

<Callout type="info" title="Pensions Outside Your Estate">
Pensions are held in trust by your pension provider. This means:
- They don't form part of your estate
- They don't count towards the Â£325,000 IHT threshold
- No 40% inheritance tax applies

This makes pensions one of the most tax-efficient assets to pass on.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="The 2-Year Rule">
For tax-free treatment, the pension scheme must designate benefits within **2 years** of death. Keep your nomination form updated to ensure this happens smoothly.

If missed, income tax applies as if you'd died after 75.
</Callout>
